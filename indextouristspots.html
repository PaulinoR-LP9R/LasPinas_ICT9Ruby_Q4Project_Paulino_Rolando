5 Best Spots in the Philippines

Hey there! Welcome to the Philippines! Now you must be lost as to where you want to go to while you stay here in the Philippines! Well you are lucky because this is a wonderful and colorful archipelago of 7,641 islands all waiting to be discovered. Lets go!

1. Calle Crisologo

Ready to be transported into the 16th Century? Look no further! Welcome to Vigan, home to Calle Crisologo, a UNESCO World Heritage Site. This 16th-Century town has grown to become a favorite among tourists. While not grand, it carries the charm it once had in the old-world during the Spanish Occupation of the Philippines. Here you can find Calesas (Horse-Drawn Carriages), their signature Ilocos Empanadas, and walk along the old cobble stone streets lined with a variety of souvenirs. If you were ever to come here, get a tour of this place in the first week of May which is when the Vigan Festival of the Arts is held.

2. Tubbataha Reef

Dive into the pristine waters of Palawan and enter Tubbataha Reef. This reef is teeming with Marine Biodiversity so much so that is listed as a UNESCO World Heritage Site like the previously mentioned Calle Crisologo. This is a top diving spot full of all sorts of life like reef sharks, manta rays, Napoleon wrasses and an incredibly amount of coral species. And if you are lucky, you can get onto one of its little islets to see a breeding site for birds and turtles. If you ever want to go diving here, make sure to plan this months or years ahead. And all that planning will pay off to witness the spectacle that the people from Palawan call Tubbataha Reef.

3. Intramuros and Fort Santiago

Walk through the wonderful ruins of the capital Manila. Walk along the streets of the 64 hectare fortress erected by Miguel Lopez de Legazpi. Amidst the bustling city rests these ruins now home to century-old churches, universities, and estabishments, and the towering citadel of Fort Santiago which rests within Intramuros which acted as a headquarters for foreign powers and their armies. This place holds historical signifiance due to it being the holding place of the national hero, Dr. Jose Rizal before he was executed.

4. Taal Volcano and Lake

Welcome to the wonderful land of Taal, Tagaytay. Home to the beauty that we call Taal Volcano. Situated within an island, within a lake, within an island sits Taal Volcano a small yet very active volcano. If you want to get away from the megacity of Manila and sit back in a cool area with amazing views and many tourist spots, head here! Don't forget to try some Bulalo while staying over in the many hotels, restaurants, and cafes that circle the wonder that is Taal Volcano.

5. Banaue Rice Terraces

Up in the land of Banaue, Ifugao sits the awe-inspiring rice terraces. (Ah yes, Rice, a staple and favorite of every Asian). This impressive place is also considered a UNESCO World Heritage Site, hand carved by the Ifugao people showcasing the ingenuity of man. You are free to gaze and view this wonderful land between the months of April-May and October-November which is peak rice growing season.
